### Research question 1: Are specific co-abundance groups (CAGs) more strongly associated with central adiposity (waist circumference) than with BMI? (Columbia dataset)
- Study conducted using subjects with an European origin, and researchers found that BMI is strongly correlated with measures of central adiposity, fat distribution, insulin resistance and dyslipidaemia. (https://doi.org/10.1111/j.2047-6310.2014.218.x)
- Study that investigated the relationship between gut microbiome and visceral fat accumulation (VFA), where researchers found that among other obese parameters such as waist, hipline, waist-to-hip ratio (WHR), and BMI, VFA has the largest number of correlation with microbial species. It also pointed out that BMI can only evaluate total body fat, but not fat distribution. (https://doi.org/10.1016/j.csbj.2020.09.026)
